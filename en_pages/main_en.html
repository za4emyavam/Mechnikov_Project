The emergence of the university

    Mechnikov Odessa National University is a leading research and educational center of modern Ukraine, the first higher education institution in the southern region of the country. The origins of university education in the south of Ukraine date back to the beginning of the XIX century, or more precisely in 1817, when the famous Richelieu Lyceum was founded.

    Having emerged as a closed educational institution for noble children, at the beginning of the third decade of its existence, thanks to the reforms of the trustee D.M. Knyazhevich, the lyceum approached the university system in terms of structure and content of education. The famous scientist M.I. Pirogov, who in the mid-1850s held the position of trustee of the Odessa educational district, gave a powerful impetus to the transformation of the lyceum into a university, and the process was completed the following decade.
